Frequently Asked Questions

When and where is it?

The hackathon is from 12 P.M. on Saturday, October 30th to 7:00 P.M. on Sunday, October 31st. Hacking will take place in the Packard Atrium. Check in will open at 12 P.M.

Who can attend?

Any and all current Stanford University students (SUID required).

What do I need to bring?

Bring your SUID, computer, chargers, and (maybe) a sleeping bag. We and our sponsors will be providing some hardware, but you’re welcome to bring your own hardware to hack on.

What if I don’t have experience?

Not a problem! We will be holding Arduino workshops before and at the event, and mentors will be available and eager to help out during the hackathon.

What can I build?

The theme of this hackathon is Earth. We're looking for novel solutions to problems that our world is facing today.

Can I work in a team?

You can work in teams of up to four people. You can come with a pre-determined group, or we can help you find one.

Will there be prizes?

To discourage hacking/making with the sole goal of deserving a prize, we will announce prizes on the day of the event.

What if I need technical support?

We will have our own truck (provided by Atmel) for all technical support. It will be located between Packard and Allen.
